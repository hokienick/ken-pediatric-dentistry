# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Parents (and guardians) of kids age 1 to 18 in San Diego, mostly mid-city neighborhoods around Kensington. The decision-maker is the parent, often researching on a phone at night after a pediatrician referral, a friend's recommendation, or a Google search for "pediatric dentist near me." They are vetting two things at once: is this practice medically credible (board-certified, safe sedation, real specialists) and will my anxious kid actually be okay here. Secondary users: existing patient families who return to find hours, the phone number, new-patient forms, and the portal.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Kensington Pediatric Dentistry and Orthodontics (kpdds.com), a real six-doctor pediatric dental and orthodontics practice at 4072 Adams Avenue, San Diego. The site's one job: convert a researching parent into a "Request an Appointment" submission or a phone call, by making the practice's genuine warmth and credentials visible. Success = appointment requests and calls; secondary success = returning families finding forms/hours/portal in one click. This replaces a DIY Squarespace build that still shows template demo content in production.

## Brand Personality

Warm, sheltering, grown-up. The brand soul is the logo: a nest with a baby bird, two parent birds holding toothbrushes, and the tagline "From first steps, till they leave the nest." The voice is a calm, experienced pediatric dentist talking to a parent: plain words, no medical jargon walls, no marketing hype, gentle humor allowed but never babyish. Kid-friendliness comes from photography (real patients, the hand-painted mural in the office) and the bird motif, never from cartoon UI or playground typography. Physical-object reference: a beloved hardcover picture book in a pediatrician's waiting room, sturdy, warm, well-made.

## Anti-references

- Generic medical-template sites: teal/cyan palette, stocky sans, icon-tile service cards (what the current site is)
- Babyish kids-brand sites: Comic Sans energy, primary-color confetti, cartoon tooth mascots
- AI-default landing pages: purple gradients, glassmorphism cards, three equal feature cards, eyebrow labels over every heading, scroll-fade-in on everything
- Editorial-magazine affectation: italic display serif + mono labels + ruled columns; this is a dental home, not a type specimen
- Corporate DSO chain-dentistry sites (bland, interchangeable, stock-photo smiles)

## Design Principles

1. **The nest is the narrative.** "From first steps, till they leave the nest" structures the site: the age-journey from first tooth to college is the signature section, the bird/branch motif is a sparse thread (max one motif moment per screen), and the footer is the nest.
2. **Credentials carry the conversion.** Diplomate status, UCLA top honors, board certifications, the dental anesthesiologist for deep sedation: presented plainly and early. Parents are vetting doctors, not buying a vibe.
3. **Real beats stock, always.** The practice has real patient photos, real headshots, a real hand-painted mural. Use them large. Never add stock imagery of fake smiling children.
4. **Calm is the aesthetic.** A worried parent should feel their shoulders drop. Generous space, soft motion, no urgency patterns, no popups, one CTA label sitewide: "Request an Appointment."
5. **Answer the night-time questions.** Hours, insurance, what happens at the first visit, is sedation safe, where do I park. If a parent has to call to ask, the page failed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum, AAA targeted for body text contrast. 16px+ body, visible 3px focus rings, 44px touch targets, semantic landmarks, skip link, full keyboard nav. prefers-reduced-motion collapses all entrance motion to static. Alt text written in brand voice for every meaningful image. Phone and SMS links are first-class (parents on phones). No emoji as icons.
